Network Analysis

Analyse how Fadigati's social network and associations change before and after the rumors about his personal life start spreading. [HERE A MORE DETAILED DEFINITION OF WHAT IS NETWORK ANALYSIS]

Research Question

Starting from our corpora (a full edition of Bassani's "Gli Occhiali d'Oro", carefully edited and tagged according to TEI guidelines), our network analysis was guided by a main research question:

How does Fadigati's network of people change before and after the discovery of his sexuality?

Connection definition

After defining the Research Question, we have worked on the definition of "connection" with regards to our project's corpora and scope. Working on a literary text would suggest that connections between characters are established when there are dialogues involving them, or when they appear in the same sentence (this situation being either a direct interaction between the two characters or an indirect one, whenever a third character compares the two).

The connections have then been defined and found through a script (python or R) in our corpora.

Graph building

After the definition of "connection" and the retrieval of such connection in the text, two different graphs were built on two different parts of the corpora. As we want to focus on how Fadigati's social network changes before and after the discovery of his sexuality, it was crucial for us to distinguish between these two moments in the literary text.

Two different sub-corpora were then created:

  • The first one spanning from Chapter 1 up to Chapter 3
  • The second one spanning from Chapter 3 to Chapter 4

The script was then run twice on these two different corpora, and the two resulting graphs were visualised by means of Gephi (? or some other tool)
