Changing Lenses

Visualising marginalisation strategies through a multilevel text analysis of "Gli occhiali d'oro" by Giorgio Bassani

Get Started

Il dottor Fadigati aveva due vite.
Ma chi non ne ha?

Starting from the text of "Gli occhiali d'oro" by Giorgio Bassani, we want to research the marginalisation and the perception of an outsider like doctor Athos Fadigati, a jewish homosexual man living in Ferrara during the Italian Fascist regime. This character is first described as a respectable professional but already at the end of the second chapter rumours start spreading:

"Non lo sai? Mi risulta che il dottor Fadigati è…" [...] "Ebbene, ho sentito dire che è…"

From that moment on, society's perception of the doctor dramatically changes.

Setting the focus

"Changing lenses" allows to read and study Bassani's "Gli occhiali d'oro" from multiple perspectives. The fil rouge of all these different approaches of is to capture all the strategies of homophobic (and also ratial) marginalisation and discrimination suffered by the protagonist and the narrator during the Fascist regime.

Semantic Digital Edition

Read the novel and explore Fadigati's world with the support of Linked Open Data.

Spatial Analysis

Scrutinize the marginalization process of the protagonist and the narrator from a geographical perspective and visualize on a map how characters inhabit the settings

Network Analysis

Visualize the relationship between characters and settings in a network and see how these change during the plot.
